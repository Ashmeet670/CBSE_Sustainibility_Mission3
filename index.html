Types of waste bins

Dry Waste

This refers to all items that are not considered wet items. This includes both recyclable and non-recyclable materials. Dry waste includes items such as bottles, cans, clothing, plastic, wood, glass, metals and paper etc.

Wet Waste

Refers to all items that are organic or kitchen waste like food items, fruit/vegetable peels, yard waste, tissues, paper towels as well as any other soiled item that would contaminate the recyclables.

Sanitary Waste

Sanitary Waste means liquid or solid wastes originating solely from humans and human activities, such as wastes collected from toilets, showers, wash basins, sinks used for cleaning domestic areas, sinks used for food preparation, clothes washing operations, and sinks

About waste segregation

What is waste segregation?

Waste segregation is the sorting and separation of waste types to facilitate recycling and correct onward disposal. These waste groups are created by looking at the characteristics of the waste; similar categories of waste are collected in the same groups

Why waste segregation?

The population of the world is increasing day by day and the amount of waste getting generated is also increasing day by day, new landfills are forming and older ones are getting bigger and bigger. Waste segregation is important not only to reduce the impact it has on the environment, but also to reduce health issues that can arise from waste and toxins that have been improperly disposed off, left to rot in the streets .
